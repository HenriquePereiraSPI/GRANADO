# What are Business Controls_

What are Business Controls?

Dassault Systèmes recognized that there are some elements of DFCs that reoccur frequently in
almost every project. As a result, a new concept was introduced called business controls. They
are similar to business components as encapsulated logic, but they boast ready-to-use graphical
user interfaces.

Business controls offer the following benefits:

           Reduced implementation costs
           Support for standardization and best practices
           Solutions to Process Builder limitations
           Pairing design with fulfillment

DELMIA Apriso comes with a set of business controls which include such options as displaying
charts/gauges, work instructions, checklists, and safety instructions. To employ these options, a
new function type called business control has been added to Process Builder.

Until now, Process authors only had inputs and outputs at their disposal when creating user
interfaces. Business controls are a new alternative that speeds up the development process by
offering an element with a pre-designed user interface which is treated similarly to inputs or outputs
– they even appear on the grid for inputs or outputs visible to the user at the step level.

Each business control consists of two parts:

           Configuration – displayed in the properties section of the business control function's
           property pane in Process Builder.
           Runtime – responsible for rendering the business control on the user's screen.
